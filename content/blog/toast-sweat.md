Most people seem not to think about toast sweat often. Like many other things, I imagine they have gotten used to having a bad time and don't care to fix it. I lived similarly for a long time.

## What even is toast sweat?

It's the moisture that forms when you place your beatiful golden brown toast ( I am partial to a 6 on the scale below) on a plate and it gets soggy.

## Why does this happen?

Simply, a difference in temperature. Your plate is too cold compared to your beautiful piece of toast. The temperature difference leads to some condensation. Something about gradients, something about energy absorbtion blah blah. Basically, you need a plate around the same temp as your toast.

## How do you fix toast sweat?

The solution is elegant. You put the plate you intend to put the toast on, on the toaster while the bread toasts. That simple. Your life imporved a great deal. NO more toast with one side wet and soggy. I am a proponenet of warm plates for food in general, small quality of life improvement. In my opinion. Something to try.

## Conclusion

I have changed your life forever, no more wet toast. Unless that is something you want to keep doing, because you hate youself.
